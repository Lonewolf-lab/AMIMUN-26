Joint Crisis Committee – Cuban Missile Crisis (1962)

About JCC - Cuban Missile Crisis

The Joint Crisis Committee recreates the tense days of the Cuban Missile Crisis, one of the most critical confrontations of the Cold War. Delegates will be divided into the cabinets of the United States and the Soviet Union, assuming the roles of key political and military leaders tasked with responding to an imminent nuclear threat. This simulation will test adaptability, strategy, and negotiation under pressure, bringing history’s closest brush with nuclear war to life.

This crisis committee will feature dynamic crisis updates, real-time decision-making, and the high-stakes diplomacy that characterized the actual event. Delegates will need to balance military strategy, diplomatic relations, and domestic political considerations as they navigate this critical moment in history.

Agenda

Topic 1

United States National Security Council: Responding to the Soviet missile deployment in Cuba

Topic 2

Soviet Politburo: Strategic positioning and response to U.S. actions

Committee Details

Committee Type: Offline

Delegation: Single Delegation

Agenda Items: 2 (Simultaneous crisis rooms)

Expected Committee Strength: 40-50 delegates (20-25 per room)

Special Note: This is a crisis committee with real-time updates and dynamic scenarios. Delegates should be prepared for fast-paced decision-making.
